Hand embroidery is a beautiful and timeless craft that can be used to add a special touch to any project. Napkin corners are a great place to start if you’re new to hand embroidery. With just a few simple stitches, you can create a unique and eye-catching design that will add a touch of elegance to your table setting.

To get started, you’ll need a few basic supplies: embroidery floss, a needle, and a piece of fabric. You can use any type of fabric you like, but a lightweight cotton or linen works best. Cut the fabric into a square that’s slightly larger than the size of your napkin. Then, thread your needle with the embroidery floss and knot the end.

Next, you’ll want to decide on a design for your napkin corner. You can use a simple stitch like a running stitch or a more complex stitch like a French knot. Once you’ve chosen your design, start stitching along the edges of the fabric. Make sure to keep your stitches even and consistent. When you’re finished, knot the end of the thread and trim the excess fabric.

Finally, you’ll need to attach the embroidered fabric to the napkin. You can do this by hand-stitching the fabric to the napkin or by using a sewing machine. Once the fabric is attached, your hand-embroidered napkin corners are ready to be used!
