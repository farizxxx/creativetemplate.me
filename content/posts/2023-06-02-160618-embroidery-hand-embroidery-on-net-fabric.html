Hand embroidery on net fabric is a unique and intricate craft that has been practiced for centuries. It is a form of needlework that involves stitching intricate designs onto a net fabric, such as tulle, organza, or lace. The fabric is usually sheer and lightweight, allowing the embroidery to be seen through the fabric. This type of embroidery is often used to create beautiful and delicate garments, such as wedding dresses, evening gowns, and lingerie. It can also be used to embellish accessories, such as handbags, hats, and scarves.

The process of hand embroidering on net fabric is quite complex and requires a great deal of skill and patience. It is important to use the right type of thread and needle for the fabric, as well as the right type of stabilizer. The stabilizer helps to keep the fabric from stretching or fraying while the embroidery is being done. It is also important to use the correct tension when stitching, as this will affect the overall look of the embroidery. Once the embroidery is complete, it is important to press the fabric to ensure that the stitches are secure and the design is even.
