Hand embroidering doll eyes is a great way to add a unique touch to your doll-making projects. It's a simple and fun technique that can be used to create a variety of different looks. With a few basic supplies and a bit of practice, you can create beautiful, one-of-a-kind eyes for your dolls.

To get started, you'll need some embroidery floss, a needle, and a pair of scissors. You'll also need a piece of felt or other fabric to use as the base for your eyes. Cut two circles out of the fabric, one slightly larger than the other. Place the smaller circle on top of the larger one and use a few stitches to secure them together.

Next, use a running stitch to outline the shape of the eye. You can use a single strand of floss or multiple strands for a thicker line. Once you've outlined the eye, use a backstitch to fill in the shape. You can use a single color or mix and match different colors to create a unique look.

Finally, use a French knot to create the pupil. To do this, wrap the floss around the needle twice and then insert the needle into the fabric. Pull the floss through and then gently tug on the ends to tighten the knot. You can also use a seed bead or other small embellishment to create the pupil.

How to Hand Embroider Doll Eyes

1. Gather supplies: embroidery floss, needle, scissors, felt or other fabric.

2. Cut two circles out of the fabric, one slightly larger than the other.

3. Place the smaller circle on top of the larger one and use a few stitches to secure them together.

4. Use a running stitch to outline the shape of the eye.

5. Use a backstitch to fill in the shape.

6. Use a French knot to create the pupil.
