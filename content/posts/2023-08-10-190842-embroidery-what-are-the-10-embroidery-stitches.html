Embroidery is an art form that has been around for centuries. It is a craft that involves stitching fabric with thread to create beautiful designs. There are many different types of embroidery stitches, each with its own unique look and purpose. In this blog post, we will explore the 10 most common embroidery stitches and how to use them.

The 10 embroidery stitches are: running stitch, back stitch, split stitch, stem stitch, chain stitch, satin stitch, French knot, lazy daisy stitch, blanket stitch, and buttonhole stitch. Each of these stitches has its own unique characteristics and uses. The running stitch is the most basic of all embroidery stitches and is used to create outlines and outlines with shading. The back stitch is a strong stitch that is used to create outlines and to join two pieces of fabric together. The split stitch is used to create outlines and to add texture to a design. The stem stitch is used to create curved lines and to add texture to a design. The chain stitch is used to create a looped line and to add texture to a design. The satin stitch is used to create a smooth, even surface and to add texture to a design. The French knot is used to create a small, raised knot and to add texture to a design. The lazy daisy stitch is used to create a looped flower and to add texture to a design. The blanket stitch is used to create a decorative edge and to add texture to a design. The buttonhole stitch is used to create a secure edge and to add texture to a design.
