How to Hand Wash Embroidery

Hand washing embroidery is a great way to keep your delicate pieces looking their best. It is important to take extra care when washing embroidery, as the fabric and threads can be easily damaged. To ensure your embroidery is properly cared for, follow these simple steps.

First, fill a sink or basin with lukewarm water and a small amount of mild detergent. Gently submerge the embroidery in the water and let it soak for a few minutes. Do not rub or scrub the fabric, as this can damage the threads. After a few minutes, drain the sink and rinse the embroidery in clean, lukewarm water. If necessary, repeat the process until all of the soap is removed.

Once the embroidery is clean, gently squeeze out the excess water and lay it flat on a clean towel. Roll the towel up and press down to remove any remaining moisture. Finally, lay the embroidery flat on a dry towel and allow it to air dry. Do not hang the embroidery to dry, as this can cause it to stretch or lose its shape.

By following these simple steps, you can keep your embroidery looking its best for years to come. With proper care and maintenance, your embroidery will remain beautiful and vibrant for many years to come.
