Shadow embroidery is a beautiful and unique way to add texture and dimension to your fabric projects. It is a type of embroidery that uses two layers of fabric to create a shadow effect. The top layer of fabric is embroidered with a design, and the bottom layer of fabric is left unembroidered. The result is a stunning 3D effect that adds depth and interest to your project.

Creating shadow embroidery by hand is a fun and rewarding experience. To get started, you will need two pieces of fabric, embroidery floss, an embroidery hoop, and a needle. Begin by placing the two pieces of fabric together, wrong sides facing each other. Secure the fabric in the embroidery hoop and use the embroidery floss to stitch the design onto the top layer of fabric. When you are finished, carefully remove the hoop and separate the two layers of fabric. The design will be visible on the top layer, and the bottom layer will remain unembroidered, creating the shadow effect.
