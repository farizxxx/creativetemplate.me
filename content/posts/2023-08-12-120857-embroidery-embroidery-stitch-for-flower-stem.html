Embroidery is a beautiful and timeless craft that has been around for centuries. It is a great way to add a unique touch to any project, and one of the most popular embroidery stitches for flower stems is the stem stitch. This stitch is perfect for creating a realistic stem for any flower, and it is easy to learn and master.

The stem stitch is worked from the bottom of the stem up, and it is created by making a series of small stitches that overlap each other. To begin, bring the needle up through the fabric and make a small stitch. Then, bring the needle back down through the fabric a short distance away from the first stitch. Make sure to keep the stitches close together, as this will create the stem. Continue making small stitches up the stem, overlapping each stitch slightly. When you reach the top of the stem, bring the needle back down through the fabric and tie off the thread.
